Key insights
OT
Overtime is the biggest risk factor
Employees working overtime have a 41.5% attrition rate vs 31.4% for those who don't — a 10.1 percentage point gap, the largest single driver in the dataset.
WLB
Poor work-life balance doubles risk
Employees rating WLB as Poor show 40.8% attrition — 6.7pp above the average and closely correlated with the overtime finding.
MAR
Single employees at higher risk
Single employees leave at 37.5% vs married at 33.7% and divorced at 33.5% — a 4pp difference, consistent with lower switching costs and flexibility to relocate.
TRV
Frequent travelers leave more often
Employees who travel frequently have 39.1% attrition vs 33.7% for those who rarely travel — a 5.4pp premium linked to burnout and WLB strain.
TEN
Long-tenured staff show rising risk
Employees with 10+ years at the company have the highest attrition rate at 35.6% — possibly driven by career plateau and lack of promotion opportunities.
EDU
High School graduates most at risk
High School education shows 35.8% attrition, the highest of any education level, potentially due to fewer internal advancement pathways and lower compensation.
